<h3> Procedure for Unipolar (Referential) Montage</h3>


<h4> 1. For Prefrontal Lobe (Fp1, Fp2)</h4>

<b>Step 1:</b>
Click on the <b>EEG cap</b> to start making connections.<br>

<b>Step 2: Establish connections</b><br>

1. Connect Fp1 lead point of human brain to the positive terminal (Fp1) of op-amp circuit.<br>
2. Connect Fp2 lead point of human brain to the positive terminal (Fp2) of op-amp circuit.<br>
3. Connect Ground lead point A1 of human brain to the negative terminal (A1) of op-amp circuit.<br>
4. Connect Ground lead point A2 of human brain to the negative terminal (A2) of op-amp circuit.<br>

<b>Step 3:</b>
Refer to the accompanying figure for correct connection points.<br>

<p align="center">
<img src="images/finalConnectionPrefrontal.png" style="width:600px; margin-top:5px; mix-blend-mode: darken;">
</p>
<br>

<b>Step 4:</b>
Select an <b>EEG Signal Mode</b> from the dropdown menu:<br>
- Normal<br>
- Eye Blink<br>
- Jaw Clenching<br>
- Forearm Muscle Contraction<br>

<b>Step 5:</b>
Click the <b>Check</b> button to verify correct connections.<br>

<b>Step 6:</b>
If the connection check indicates an error, click the <b>Reset</b> button.<br>

<b>Step 7:</b>
Click the <b>Show Signal</b> button to display the simulated EEG waveform.<br>

<b>Step 8:</b>
Click the <b>Print</b> button to save the recorded observations.<br>

<b>Step 9:</b>
Select another lobe from the left panel to record EEG Signals for its corresponding lobes.<br>
